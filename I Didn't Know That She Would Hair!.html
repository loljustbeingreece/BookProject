I Didn't Know That She Would Hair!

|

Meg March

Desperate times call for desperate measures. 

 Recently, my family received a telegram saying that my father was seriously injured in the war. Naturally, my sisters and I helped out every possible way to help my mother make ends meet (Alcott, 213). That means we pick up chores, ask other people for help, or cut off all of our hair and sell it to a barber shop. 

Yes, you read that correctly. My sister, Josephine March, cut off her hair and sold it so it could be made into a wig.

She received about twenty-five dollars for her hair, even though it was an undesirable color (215). Thanks to the barber shop owner and his wife, our father's medical bills will not be super difficult to pay. 

I was incredibly surprised by Jo chopping off her hair, even though she is rather tomboyish. 

Apparently, she was too.

After the scissors split her hair, she was up late crying (217). I didn't expect her to have such an attachment. She easily overcame her brief sadness, and she would most likely cut it again in a heart beat (219).
